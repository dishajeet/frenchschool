How We Began

A Certified Approach to Learning

École Canada French was founded by Catherine Dubois, a native of Montreal with a Master's degree in Applied Linguistics. After years of conducting official TEF and TCF exams in Canada, Catherine noticed that general language classrooms failed to support students with critical, time-sensitive deadlines.

Whether a student needed to reach CLB 7 for an immigration file, keep their child from falling behind in French immersion, or build travel confidence, they were all grouped together in the same casual textbook classes. This lack of specialization cost students time and money.

We established our school to offer specialized online French tutoring in Canada. By building distinct pathways for exam preparation, children's homework, and adult conversational fluency, we make sure every minute of live instruction directly advances your target milestones.

Catherine Dubois, Founder & Lead TCF/TEF Instructor
Our Methodology

Three Pillars of Our Philosophy

We believe language learning should be outcome-focused, curriculum-aligned, and deeply supported by expert educators.

1. Outcome-Driven Customization

We don't believe in generic syllabuses. Every student begins with a private assessment session, establishing specific milestones (like exam points or reading speeds) which govern our lesson plans.

2. Specialized Native Instructors

Our educators aren't just native French speakers—they are certified teachers, official exam evaluators, and school board curriculum specialists who know exactly how to teach French online.

3. Bilingual Accountability

We maintain full transparency. Adult students receive digital review logs after each lesson, and parents receive bilingual feedback notes in English to track their children's progress effortlessly.

Bilingualism in Canada

Why French Matters in the Canadian Context

In Canada, French is more than just a heritage language—it is a critical tool for professional advancement, immigration advantage, and educational success.

🇨🇦 Immigration Advantages (CRS Points)

Under Canada's Express Entry system, candidates who prove French language proficiency (scoring CLB 7 or higher on the TEF/TCF exam) can claim up to 50 additional CRS points. This score boost often represents the margin needed to receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residency.

🎒 K-12 Immersion Success

Enrollment in French Immersion schools has grown rapidly across Canada. However, research shows that children succeed most when they have regular reinforcement at home. Our curriculum-aligned homework support bridges the gap for English-speaking parents, ensuring kids build long-term bilingual literacy.

💼 Career & Public Sector Jobs

Bilingualism opens doors to lucrative federal public service positions, international relations opportunities, and bilingual corporate roles in major hubs like Montreal, Ottawa, and Toronto. Learning French expands your professional footprint and makes you highly competitive in the job market.
