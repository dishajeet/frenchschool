Common Questions

TEF / TCF Canada Prep FAQ

Everything you need to know about immigration French exams and our training methodology.

What is the difference between TEF Canada and TCF Canada?

+
Both exams are fully accepted by IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) for Express Entry and Quebec immigration. The core difference lies in formatting: TEF has slightly different grading metrics for writing and multiple-choice scoring penalties, while TCF is entirely computerized with slightly shorter sections. Our tutors prepare you for whichever exam you choose to book, and we will help you decide which one matches your skills during your free assessment.

What French score level do I need for Express Entry CRS points?

+
To claim additional bilingual points under the Express Entry system, you must score a minimum of NCLC/CLB 7 across all four skills (Speaking, Writing, Reading, and Listening). A CLB 7 score gives you up to 50 additional CRS points directly, which can be the deciding factor in receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

How many hours of study do I need to reach CLB 7?

+
This depends on your starting level. If you are an absolute beginner, reaching CLB 7 typically requires 250–350 hours of structured, focused learning. If you have some high-school French background (B1 level), we can often get you to CLB 7 in 60–100 hours of intensive training, focusing heavily on exam techniques and speaking mock exercises.

Are mock exams and scoring materials included in the price?

+
Yes. Unlike other general language schools, all our immigration exam prep packages include full, unlimited access to our mock exam templates, exam-day checklists, vocabulary cheat sheets, and evaluation rubrics at no extra charge.
